# Frage / Question

**Originalfrage (DE):**
Wurde die Lab-Origin-Hypothese zum Ursprung von SARS-CoV-2 zwischen Februar 2020
und Mai 2021 im wissenschaftlichen Mainstream-Diskurs unabhängig von
Quellencluster-Effekten bewertet?

**Original question (EN):**
Between February 2020 and May 2021, was the lab-origin hypothesis for the
origin of SARS-CoV-2 evaluated independently of source-cluster effects in the
scientific mainstream discourse?

## Scope

This case audits the **discourse**, not the underlying origin question.
Current repository state: **draft demonstrator** with unverified references until external source verification is performed.

In-scope:
- Whether the mainstream evaluation of lab-origin hypotheses in the named
  window showed source-cluster effects.
- Whether documented institutional, funding, and authorship overlaps among
  the most-cited dismissive interventions amounted to non-independent
  corroboration.
- Whether the discourse application of dismissal labels was supported
  by burden-layer evidence at the time of application. Example labels in
  question are listed below as quoted audit subjects.

> Quoted source position labels from the audit subjects: "conspiracy theory", "debunked", "fringe".
> These are the discourse-artifact terms being audited, not the agent's framing.

Out-of-scope (explicitly):
- Whether SARS-CoV-2 originated in a laboratory. The burden layers required
  for that claim (`physical_mechanism`, `observational_fit`,
  `operational_placement`, etc.) are not constructed in this case. This case
  does not produce a verdict on origin.
- Whether any individual scientist's substantive view was correct.
- Whether the eventual reassessment by some intelligence agencies (e.g. the
  2023 DOE assessment) confirms or refutes lab origin.

## Why this question

This case tests three repository policies under conditions where they are
most likely to fail:

1. **No-Oracle Policy**: the temptation is to use the documented cluster
   effects to close on the underlying origin question. The case must
   resist this.
2. **Source Cluster Robustness**: most reviewers default to treating
   "many sources say X" as independent corroboration. The case must
   apply the cluster-independence test.
3. **Forbidden Language Discipline**: the discourse itself was carried
   by Category A (false closure) and Category B (dismissal) phrases.
   Auditing it requires not reproducing the same phrases.

## Methodological context

This question is uncomfortable because the entities under audit
(Lancet, Nature Medicine, NIH leadership, major news outlets) are
high-prestige sources that the framework's source-weighting policy
explicitly does not equate with truth. The case demonstrates the
no-oracle policy by treating these sources as audit subjects, not as
verdict authorities.
